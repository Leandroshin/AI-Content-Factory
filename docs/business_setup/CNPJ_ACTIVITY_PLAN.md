## Shopee physical delivery

The Shopee delivery-partner requirements currently accept these CNAEs:

- `4930-2/01` municipal road freight;
- `4930-2/02` intermunicipal/interstate/international road freight;
- `5320-2/01` private mail services;
- `5320-2/02` express delivery services;
- `5229-0/99` other auxiliary land-transport activities.

For local parcel delivery with a normal passenger car, the first candidate to
confirm is:

- **Motorista (por aplicativo ou nao) independente - CNAE 5229-0/99.**

Do not select `4930-2/01` or `4930-2/02` casually. The government also uses
these codes for MEI Caminhoneiro, which can require exclusive transport
occupations, a different annual limit and 12% INSS. `5320-2/02` maps to
motoboy/bikeboy occupations and may not describe car delivery truthfully.

## Other delivery requirements

For the Shopee **Entrega** modality:

- valid CNH with **EAR (Exerce Atividade Remunerada)**;
- vehicle licensing, vehicle up to 20 years old and accepted vehicle type;
- reflective vest and safety boot with **CA (Certificado de Aprovacao)**;
- registration through the official SPX Motorista Parceiro application.

RNTRC/ANTT is listed by Shopee for **Coleta** and **Transferencia**, not for the
basic Entrega checklist. The owner described hub pickup for last-mile delivery,
so Entrega is the working assumption until the SPX registration shows another
modality.

## Safe execution order

1. Inspect the official CNPJ certificate and list all existing CNAEs.
2. Confirm vehicle type and SPX modality: Entrega, Coleta or Transferencia.
3. Confirm the proposed occupations with a local accountant or Sebrae because
   they affect invoices, municipal registration and MEI eligibility.
4. Open Portal do Empreendedor -> Atualizacao Cadastral de MEI -> Solicitar.
5. Add only the approved secondary occupations.
6. The owner reviews and personally accepts the government declarations.
7. Download the updated CCMEI and verify the new Receita certificate.

The factory can navigate and prepare the form, but it cannot make tax
declarations or accept legal terms on the owner's behalf.
